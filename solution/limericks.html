Solution: Limericks
Answer: QUALIFIER

Written by Kat Fang, Jinna

Flipping the last two letters of each word, we get the two limerick riddles:

The masked and New Zealand have ailed.
But not the common or scaled.
The buttons are fake.
Fried eggs kwek-kwek make.
What creature have we surveilled?

If you add me to cracker or fly,
You will get pretty lights in the sky.
If you make me a wall,
I will filter it all.
Can you figure me out? What am I?

The answer to the first riddle is “quail,” as the masked bobwhite quail is endangered and the New Zealand quail is now extinct, while the common quail and scaled quail are not endangered. Kwek-kwek is a dish made of fried quail eggs.

The answer to the second is “fire” — adding fire to fly and cracker gives us firefly and firecracker, which are both lights in the sky, and adding it to wall gives us “firewall.”

We then flip the last two letters of both words, as done in the original verses, to get “quali” and “fier.” The answer is qualifier.
