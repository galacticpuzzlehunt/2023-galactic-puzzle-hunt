Solution: The Swarm

Written by Lewis Chen

The grid has 16 rows and 5 columns. The back 7 rows are entirely filled by Bee Riders, except for the 1st, 3rd, and 5th cells in the back row, which contain Yurts. On our first row, there are Camps in the 1st, 3rd, and 5th cells.

We can start by hitting end turn to observe the Bee Riders’ behavior. On the 1st enemy turn, all of the Bee Riders move forward 2 spaces (Flex and Move). The 2nd enemy turn generates Bee Riders on the 2nd and 4th cells of row 2, and a full row of Bee Riders on row 3. Subsequent turns move existing Bee Riders forward 2 spaces and generate more Bee Riders 2 rows at a time, forming an alternating checkerboard pattern. Bee Riders only attack targets that are adjacent to them after their move phase (including diagonals), although they prefer to move forward if possible.

As we play through this battle, we may notice that units can affect targets at most 5 spaces in front of themselves. This makes it difficult for us to damage the opponent’s Yurts. It also limits the ranges of card effects we might like to use; for example, the Oklahoma Tusks Special only reaches enemies at most 5 rows away.

We need to find a way to clear Bee Riders quickly! Otherwise, they’ll advance to the end of the row, then start damaging our Camps and cards.

Looking through the deck for cards to use, we find Quail Aboard Fiery Steeds. Placing one in our 2nd column and another in our 4th column allows us to quickly clear the Bee Riders in our Space (those cards become Sneaky when they enter our Space, which means none of them are protected!).

Quail Aboard Fiery Steeds is kind of expensive to play, and its Special costs 2 Boarries, so we need some other red cards to support it. Cheap ones that we can play during the first turns will allow us to ramp up our Boarry production. We can also include some other cards with useful abilities.

We spend a turn using Quail Aboard Fiery Steeds to get a bit of breathing room. After that, we need to advance our units far enough into the enemy Space that they can damage the Yurts. Specifically, units need to be in the 7th row (the “2nd row inside” the opponent’s Space). Note that these units become Sneaky when they enter enemy Space, so we don’t have to clear all of the Bee Riders in front of the target Yurt.

After destroying all the Yurts, it turns out the battle isn’t over! Thankfully, because the Yurts are destroyed, no more Bee Riders get generated. We are able to defeat all the remaining Bee Riders pretty easily, and then the battle ends.

One example of a deck that can beat The Swarm is:

2× Hamlet
2× Hog
2× Oklahoma Tusks
2× Quail Aboard Fiery Steeds
2× Switch Boar’d
