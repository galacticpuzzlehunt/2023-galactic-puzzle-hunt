Solution: Kero

Written by Colin Lu

As mentioned in the preparation screen and cutscene text, this puzzle allows (and requires) the use of all of the legendary cards.

After experimenting with some combinations of the legendary cards, we will find that there are some extremely powerful combos involving two legendary cards. Furthermore, from reading the Kero Dos text, we can determine that there are six “waves” of enemies in total. This makes it natural to try to come up with six two-card combos to use the 12 legendary cards in.

There are some additional clues that can help narrow down where each card needs to be used: Slime and Moonick enable us to draw through most of the deck, which is most useful early on, and Mister Penny destroys our own bases, which would suggest it should be used last.

In addition to the legendary cards, across the battle, six small Creatures are needed in order to get enough Food to play the legendaries. Thus, an example 30-card deck could be:

  • 13 Slime, Duplication Glitch
  • 1 of each other legendary (11 total)
  • 2 Flora the Explora
  • 2 re
  • 2 Log

With a deck like this, no special abilities of the non-legendary cards are needed, and there is some leeway for slightly sub-optimal play routes.

Dinosaur Tail

Legendaries used: 13 Slime, Duplication Glitchs and Moonick Non-legendaries used: 1 re

Dinosaur Tail’s passive suggests summoning many Creatures will be useful in order to deal enough damage. After playing any small Creature (eg. re) on turn one, playing Moonick, followed by Slime, Duplication Glitch, will (very likely) fill the board with Slime, Duplication Glitchs and draw almost the entire deck. This gives two turns to attack with a full board, which will deal enough damage to defeat Dinosaur Tail (with some Food left over to finish drawing the deck).

In order to gain more consistency in this phase, it’s helpful (although not necessary) to use the Cardinal Director mastery to ensure Moonick is in the starting hand. Note that the same general strategy will work with fewer than 13 Slime, Duplication Glitchs, but the probability of the combo whiffing and not filling the board with enough Slimes increases dramatically as the number of Slimes in the deck decreases.

Boar Tusks

Legendaries used: Coloring and Othello

This stage only gives us two turns, which suggests that using a card that can enable attacks on the turn it’s summoned, namely Coloring. In fact, Coloring and Othello can be used together to summon arbitrarily many copies of Coloring, by using the Flex of each newly summoned Coloring on Othello, and then copying another Coloring. These copies of Coloring can then attack immediately at no cost.

On the first turn, we need to summon three small Creatures (eg. 2 Flora the Explora, 1 re) to generate either Whipped Creams or Flowers, so that Coloring and Othello can be summoned together on turn two.

Dryad Horns

Legendaries used: Jabberwock and Dargle

Dargle's text says "When this Creature deals Damage, it deals that amount of Damage to itself, and increases its Power by the same amount. Jabberwock reduces one instance of Damage to 0, since the smybol § gives the absolute difference between the next two values, and the next two symbols are the same.

Dryad Horns have a huge amount of Health, suggesting exponential growth will be useful. On turn one, summon Dargle. On turn two, Summon Jabberwock and attack with Dargle, then use Jabberwock's Special on Dargle and Attack with Dargle until Dryad Horns is defeated.

Cow Legs and Cow Udder

Legendaries used: Miss Yu and bb b

The full board of enemies suggests a card that can attack multiple times such as Miss Yu will be useful. By using Miss Yu in combination with bb b, Miss Yu can sweep through the entire board of Cow Legs in one turn, and then defeat Cow Udder.

Bee Wings

Legendaries used: Blancmange and Spelling Bee

Bee Wings are immune to most methods of dealing damage, but a “donated” Spelling Bee can eliminate it. One additional small Creature (eg. Log) is needed to help generate enough Food to play Blancmange on turn two.

Kero

Legendaries used: Captain Pi and Mister Penny

Kero’s ability requires us to do a very large amount of damage at once, which Captain Pi can help with.

After playing any small Creature on turn one (eg. Log), we can summon Mister Penny on turn two, and Captain Pi on turn three by using Mister Penny’s Flex. Captain Pi can then simply attack twice for the win.

Alternate Strategy

Boar Tusks can be defeated by an alternate strategy, using Mister Penny to ramp out Queen of Bison and Coloring on turn two to enable Queen of Bison to attack immediately. Teams that do this will naturally attempt to finish the battle from this state.

After progressing through the other phases as in the above solution, we then have access to Captain Pi and Othello to defeat Kero. Othello can be used to enable Captain Pi to attack twice on the turn after it is summoned, so we need some way to summon Captain Pi on turn four. By packing our deck with highly efficient ways to generate Food, this can barely be done, but requires using the minimum number of Slime, Duplication Glitch (so that more efficient Food generating Creatures can be added to the deck), and more specific plays during the other segments.

An example deck for this strategy:

  • 8 Slime, Duplication Glitch
  • 1 of each other legendary (11 total)
  • 2 Chicken
  • 2 Glass of Moo
  • 1 Glass of Moocha
  • 2 Hamlet
  • 2 Hog
  • 1 Hog Hog
  • 1 Queen of Bison

This strategy has significant overlap with the "standard" approach, but does require more specific conditions to be met at various points, so an example full fight plan is below:

  • Dinosaur Tail
    • Use Cardinal Director to ensure Moonick is in the starting hand.
    • Restart until exactly one Slime, Duplication Glitch is in hand, as well as a Hamlet or Hog.
    • Turn 1: Play Hamlet or Hog.
    • Turn 2: Play Moonick.
    • Turn 3: Play Slime, Duplication Glitch.
    • Repeat the above steps until all eight Slime, Duplication Glitch are summoned onto the board. Making use of all three Instancers as well as the main puzzle can speed this part up.
    • Turn 4, 5: Attack with all Creatures.
  • Boar Tusks
    • Note that due to having fewer Slimes, this setup does not draw the full deck, so occasionally a reset will be needed here (or at the start of the next wave), if the required cards are not in hand.
    • Turn 1: Play Hog in the middle column. Play Hamlet anywhere. Play Hamlet or Hog anywhere (whichever one wasn't played on Turn 1 during Dinosaur Tail.
    • Turn 2: Create using Hamlet and the last Creature played during Turn 1. Play Mister Penny with 1 Food left over. Use Mister Penny's Flex 9 times (3 on each Base) to generate 9 Food. Play Queen of Bison and Coloring (uses 8 Food, leaving 2 Food left over). Use Coloring's Flex on Queen of Bison. Attack with Queen of Bison and the Hog in the middle column.
  • Dryad Horns
    • Turn 1: Play Dargle.
    • Turn 2: Attack with Dargle and play Jabberwock.
    • Turn 3+: Use Jabberwock's Special on Dargle and attack with Dargle.
  • Cow Legs and Cow Udder
    • Turn 1: Play Miss Yu.
    • Turn 2: Play bb b next to Miss Yu. Attack all of the Cow Legs with Miss Yu, then attack Cow Udder.
    • Turn 3: Attack Cow Udder with Miss Yu.
  • Bee Wings
    • Turn 1: Play Hog Hog in R4C2. Play Spelling Bee in R4C3.
    • Turn 2: Play Blancmange. Use Blancmange's Flex on Spelling Bee, moving it to R3C3. Spelling Bee's damage will destroy both Bee Wings and Hog Hog, giving a Hog in hand.
  • Kero
    • Turn 1: Play 2 Chicken and 1 Hog (generated from the Hog Hog in the previous phase).
    • Turn 2: Create with Hog. Play Othello. Use 2 Chicken Specials.
    • Turn 3: Use 2 Chicken Specials. Create with Hog. Play 2 Glass of Moo and 1 Glass of Moocha
    • Turn 4: Create with 2 Chicken, 1 Hog, 1 Othello. Use 2 Glass of Moo Special and 1 Glass of Moocha Special. Play Captain Pi.
    • Turn 5: Use Othello's Special on Captain Pi. Attack with both Captain Pi and the copied Captain Pi.

This strategy can also work with some slight modifications, but requires similar tricks like using Glass of Moo and Chicken to generate extra Food, and sacrificing Hog Hog after using it to generate Food in order to achieve more card efficiency.

In fact, it's possible to generate so much Food that it is possible to attack 3 times with Captain Pi, which is a fun challenge.

See solution:
  • Bring an additional Hog Hog, replacing a Hog, and use the Hog Hog instead of the Hog against Boar Tusks. This will leave an additional Hog in hand.
  • Against Kero, instead do the following:
    • Turn 1: Play 2 Chicken and 1 Hog.
    • Turn 2: Create with Hog. Play Othello. Use 2 Chicken Specials.
    • Turn 3: Use 2 Chicken Specials. Create with Hog and Othello. Play 2 Glass of Moo, 1 Glass of Moocha, and 1 Hog.
    • Turn 4: Create with 2 Chicken, 2 Hog. Use 2 Glass of Moo Special and 1 Glass of Moocha Special. Play Captain Pi. Use Othello Special on Captain Pi and attack with the copied Captain Pi.
    • Turn 5: Use Othello's Special on Captain Pi. Attack with both Captain Pi and the copied Captain Pi.

Author’s Notes

Early on in the hunt writing process, there were various discussions about what to do as a “capstone” puzzle for this hunt, since a traditional meta wouldn’t be possible (since the card battles don’t have answers). I came up with this “final battle” requiring cards obtained over the course of the hunt as an alternative. I thought that allowing players to execute really "broken" combos would feel like an epic conclusion to the hunt.

Since some of the combos would trivialize other puzzles, I added the restriction that players can only use one legendary card in their deck outside of this final battle.

An earlier version of this puzzle had a version of Boar Tusks with much more Health, since the combo intended to be used for that part can deal arbitrary amounts of damage. However, I lowered its Health so that players wouldn't have to press as many buttons to repeatedly execute the combo. This enabled the alternative solution with Mister Penny, Coloring, and Queen of Bison. I decided to keep this in, because in testing it enabled teams to see through to the end of the puzzle, at which point they saw the Captain Pi + Mister Penny combo for Kero, and then deduced that they should use Coloring and Othello instead for Boar Tusks.

I was a little surprised that some teams pushed through and made the Alternate Strategy above work, but it seemed that teams that opted for this approach really enjoyed executing the Alternate Strategy, so I'm glad it was available to them.

Fans of Magic: The Gathering might notice that some of the card combinations and effects are quite similar to some well known MTG combos. In particular, the Coloring + Othello combination is similar to the Splinter Twin combo (with Pestermite, or a similar card), and the Slime, Duplication Glitch + Moonick combination was inspired by MTG’s “ripple” mechanic. An earlier concept for Blancmange was similar to Phyrexian Dreadnought.

Lastly, I really want to thank DD for her work on the animations and art for this puzzle, which really brought it to life.
