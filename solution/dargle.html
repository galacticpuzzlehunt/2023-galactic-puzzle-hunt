Solution: Dargle

Written by Colin Lu, Anderson Wang

In this battle, we need to battle enemies with powerful effects, where the card text has been altered so that generally each word is only visible up to the first vowel.

We should think about common keywords and possible card effects to figure out exactly what the effects are. Once we know what the card effects are, the stat lines of the enemy Creatures are generally not too threatening in this puzzle.

Enemy Descriptions and Strategies

Enemy Plain text effect Strategy Notes
Cargle If any Enemy Creature would deal Damage from the first or last column, Destroy it instead.
Dorgle If any Enemy Creature would deal Damage from the second or fourth column, Destroy it instead. Most Creatures can handle Cargle and Dorgle without issues.
Morgle This Creature can only be Attacked if another Creature moved into its column this turn. This uses a very generous interpretation of the text, in which moving up and down within Morgle’s column counts.

Moving a pair of Creatures back and forth in the front row of the player area while attacking with a unit behind them is a safe way to defeat Morgle.
Corgle When this Creature is Damaged, Destroy the Attacking Creature unless the player controlling it has exactly three Food afterwards.
Bargle Base.
At the beginning of your turn, if this Base’s Current Health is not prime, destroy all Enemy Units.		 For this enemy, we can ramp up for some very strong attacks in later turns, or quickly start attacking with 6 Damage.
Durgle This Creature does not take Damage from non-Dinosaur Creatures.
Gorgle This Creature takes ten times as much Damage when it is Attacked by Creatures with at least four Current Health.
Swargle If this Creature takes Damage, Destroy all Enemy Units.
This Creature is Destroyed automatically in six turns.		 For this last wave, Dinosaurs and Creatures with high Health (e.g., Yoshisaur Munchakoopas) are particularly useful.

Author’s Notes

This puzzle was originally written before the “sub-battle” concept was introduced, so the player needed to defeat every wave of enemies in a row, with the player’s board being wiped of Creatures in between.

This version was quite difficult in testing (since the player can’t really afford to lose any bases in between rounds, and needs a more optimized deck to handle every wave with the same deck), so we converted it to a series of sub-battles, which we felt was much more in line with its current place in the puzzle order.

This puzzle was inspired by Rankle and Torbrankle.
