Solution: Trigram Hell
Answer: PERMANENT

Written by Brian Chen

By taking one word from each of the three columns in order, solvers can assemble them into nine three-word clues. These clues solve to three nine-letter words and six three-letter words.

  • had a meal = ATE
  • handle subordinates poorly = MISMANAGE
  • in favor of = FOR
  • ketchup or mustard = CONDIMENT
  • make small holes = PERFORATE
  • mediants in solfege = MIS
  • not well lit = DIM
  • opposite of pro = CON
  • years since birth = AGE

If the nine-letter words are split into trigrams, two of the three trigrams appear among the other answers (which also helps clarify any ambiguities). Of the remaining trigrams, one is the first trigram in its word, one is the second, and one is the third:

PER FOR ATE
MIS MAN AGE
CON DIM ENT

Those trigrams form the answer, PERMANENT.
