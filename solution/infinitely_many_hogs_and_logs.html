Solution: Infinitely Many Hogs (and Logs)

Written by Violet Xiao

This battle contains 4 sub-battles that involve various combinations of factories, logs, hogs, and stacking.

Battle 1

This battle only requires us to be able to deal with one Hog and one Log, after one setup turn. There are many ways to go about this. For example, we could play a Beeowulf and a Beethoven on turn 1, then on turn 2, use them to destroy the Hog and Log, then spend our remaining food to summon a Hamlet, then in subsequent turns use the Beeowulf and Beethoven to remove the new Hog and Log, while using the Hamlet to whittle down the enemy Factories.

Battle 2

In this battle, we face a Hog Factory, a Log Factory, and a Stacktory. If all three of the enemy bases are alive, the AI alternates between summoning a Hog on a Log and a Log on a Hog. If the Stacktory is not alive, any remaining factories spawn a Creature just like in Battle 1. If the Stacktory is alive but one or more of the other factories have been removed, the Stacktory has no effect, and any remaining factories spawn like in Battle 1.

In this battle, since the AI starts with a Hog on a Log on our turn 1, we start off on the back foot, at least relative to the previous battle. One strategy to beat this battle is to rush down the enemy Log Factory and destroy it on turn 2, since the AI doesn’t guard that column by then. This can be done by playing two Hogs (and another unit) on turn 1, then using the Hogs to attack down the Log Factory on turn 2. Once this is done, we only need to deal with one Hog per turn. This can be done with cheap units in general (since we can play more than one per turn, we’ll amass a lead), and it especially helps to have units such as PBee & J or Beethoven.

Battle 3

In this battle, we are faced with a Hog Factorytorytory, and a Log Factorytorytory. A Factorytorytory spawns a Factorytory, a Factorytory spawns a Factory, and a Factory spawns a Creature (Hog or Log) of the corresponding type. The AI’s Factories spawn in top-to-bottom, left-to-right order, and only spawn on their side of the board.

Though the AI can potentially summon a lot of units this way, if left to its own devices, it will only summon one Creature on its side of the board, and fill the rest of its spaces with various types of factories, thanks to the spawning order. If we fill up a single enemy space before that point, it won’t even have space to summon any Creatures at all! If we do that, we will have unlimited time to set up our side of the field however we’d like. To proceed from there, we can either remove the AI’s factories a couple at a time, filling up the empty spaces with our own Creatures to prevent more from being spawned, or we can build up a powerful set of forces that can overwhelm the rate that the AI can respawn things (which is capped at 7 units per turn). Creatures such as Quail Aboard Fiery Steeds and Nice Cow can help with this strategy.

Battle 4

In this battle, the AI spawns units that are essentially an infinite stack: the Hog on a Log on a... will spawn a Log on a Hog on a... if destroyed, and vice-versa. However, the enemy factory will only spawn on the same column that it’s on, so the total number of enemy units out at a time can be limited a lot more, provided those units never move.

One strategy to deal with this battle is to immediately fill up an enemy space in the middle column, limiting the AI to a single Creature, then building up enough power (while just tanking the 1 Creature’s worth of damage every turn) to kill the enemy base with either Murray or with some Sneaky units. We can deal with the single Creature either by repeatedly playing low cost units in front of the enemy Hog on a Log on a..., or by using Vanilla Calf and spending 2 Food to heal off its damage every turn.

Author’s Notes

This idea for this battle started out with Kat saying that she wished she could draw a hog on a log as card art. I proposed the Hog on a Log and Log on a Hog cards. I thought that the stacking nature of the cards was really amusing, so I made some levels with more and more ridiculous levels of stacking.

Many thanks to Lewis for the card art in this battle, and also many thanks to DD for drawing the map page art for this battle, and Alan for writing the special case code to get it on the map page. It was a lot of effort for one little gag, but I think it really added a lot of personality to the hunt!
