Solution: Mister Penny

Written by Amon Ge

Mister Penny’s Mints aren’t minting coins, but colorful paper Creatures. Also, notice that they all have 2 Power, but varying Health. For instance, Spicy Fermented Cabbage has 5000 Health! The names of the Creatures each clue a country: the aforementioned card clues kimchi, which is Korean.

Googling “Korean 5000” will show a red 5000 KRW banknote, and comparing with the other bills reveals the mechanic of this battle:

Each Creature's name is cluing a particular country with colorful bill denominations (unrelated to the design of any banknote). They share the color and health of a particular banknote from that country. They also take damage accordingly to their currency – both a yellow Bee and a green Dinosaur will destroy Spicy Fermented Cabbage in one Attack, dealing 50000 and 10000 damage respectively. There aren’t blue cards, so they’re ignored.

The strategy to win the battle is to play a variety of colorful Creatures without consideration for their base Power. Attacking an enemy Creature with any color of a higher denomination for their respective currency instantly destroys them.

After dealing with the Creatures on the board, we’ll have a few leftover Attacks to start taking down the Mints. Eventually, we can destroy all the Mints and befriend Mister Penny!

Author’s Notes

This puzzle is an attempt to counter all the US-centric puzzles out there. Imagine having to distinguish your bills by faces instead of colour!

Mister Penny actually comes from an extremely half-baked game jam game S and I made many years ago (arrow keys to move, space to use part of yourself as bait). The best part of that demo is the art and music. I slapped some boar ears and tusks onto Mister Penny as placeholder art. It never changed.

Mister Penny is married to Miss Yu. Their four-year-old son, Kai Yu, is a bald human.
