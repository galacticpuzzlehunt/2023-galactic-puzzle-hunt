Solution: Coloring

Written by Kevin Matthew Li

This puzzle is about the fact that Colorado and Wyoming are the same shape—in fact, they are each defined to be exactly 7 degrees of longitude wide and 4 degrees of latitude tall, and are grid-aligned; that is, their boundaries are along integer meridians and parallels.

There are 8 cards, each of which is owned by both players. Each of them is a place name located in both Colorado and Wyoming. In the case where there is ambiguity among the same place name within one of the states, the flavor can help to disambiguate which one is correct.

Place name CO lat CO lon WY lat WY lon
Arvada 39.803 −105.087 44.654 −106.131
Burlington 39.304 −102.269 44.446 −108.431
Centennial 39.596 −104.844 41.298 −106.138
Cow Butte 38.457 −103.624 41.652 −107.664
Finger Rock 40.127 −106.911 44.551 −107.193
Grover 40.868 −104.226 42.793 −110.932
Ramsey Peak 40.525 −105.615 41.173 −109.261
Saddle Mountain 38.705 −107.516 44.710 −109.984

The first three are Bases, while the others are Creatures. It doesn’t mean much that they are different—in this battle, the Bases are mobile.

Because the battle grid is 4 high and 7 wide, each cell corresponds to a 1×1 latitude-longitude grid cell in the real world, whether in Colorado or Wyoming. For example, the southwestern corner of Colorado is (37, −109). Arvada, Colorado’s coordinates are (39.8, −105.1), so that means it is on the third row from the bottom, and the fourth column from the left.

In this battle, the opponent is Colorado and we are Wyoming. The opponent starts their Creatures and Bases in the correct corresponding locations for Colorado:

The goal is to, upon defeating all the enemy Creatures and Bases, have our Creatures and Bases in the corresponding locations for Wyoming:

Note that in order to win, all Units must be kept alive, since they’re all required to set up the final formation. Also, the enemy does not begin attacking until one of their Units is destroyed, which we can take advantage of by getting some initial damage in against the Bases.

To win the battle, one basic strategy is

  • Place three Creatures, each defended by one of the Bases.
  • Attack to reduce all enemy Bases to 1 Current Health.
  • Attack Saddle Mountain, Cow Butte, and Finger Rock (the former two must be defeated to prevent Sneaky attacks). The enemy Attack will deal 2 damage to a Base.
  • Attack Arvada, Ramsey Peak, and Centennial. The enemy Attack will deal 1 damage to a Base, leaving it on 1 Current Health.
  • Attack Grover and Burlington.

This leaves as much time as needed to play the remaining Creatures and move into the final formation, and befriend Coloring.

Note that it is not necessary to actually eliminate all of the enemy Units—for instance, Burlington can be left alive at the end of the above Strategy, since it's a Base that won't attack back.

Author’s Notes

This puzzle was entirely motivated by a dumb pun: "Coloring" is a portmanteau of Colorado and Wyoming. Hope you groaned.
