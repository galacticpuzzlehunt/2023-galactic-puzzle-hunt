Solution: Spelling Bee

Written by Josh Alman, Jenna Himawan, and Nathan Pinsker

When we open this puzzle, we can make a few observations right away:

  • The grid has 6 rows and 8 columns.
  • We have a base, the Beehive, with 1 health.
  • The AI has a base, the Bearhive, with 99 health. The Bearhive text says that it contains 0 happy bears and 0 sad bears.
  • The flavortext says “We’ll keep the H, O, N, E, Y fresh, so it won’t reset between T, U, R, N, S!”
  • We start with 15 honey.
  • Our deck contains 49 cards.
  • Our hand has 5 Larva cards. Larvas cannot Attack, Create, or Move. Their Special (costing 1 honey) is to turn into a Bee. They also produce one honey at the start of our turn.

If we progress through the battle, we’ll see the following:

  • Bears will sometimes spawn in the grid. Each bear “wants to see” a certain kind of thing. For example, the first bear to appear, the Luge Bear, “wants to see a winter Olympic sport before it enters the Bearhive next turn”.
  • The turn after the bear appears, it will “enter the Bearhive”, and the Bearhive’s counts of happy and sad bears will change accordingly.

We can also investigate our cards and what they can do.

  • The Larva Special allows it to turn into 26 different types of Bee, one for each letter of the alphabet.
  • Bees also cannot Attack, Create, or Move, and they generate honey at the start of the turn. The Bee Flex ability destroys the Bee.

The next question is how to make the bears happy. Given the title, flavortext, and Larva / Bee abilities, we might try spelling members of the categories that the bears want to see. As a simple example, if we use our bees to spell LUGE on Turn 2, we can see that the Bearhive counter goes up when the Luge Bear enters it.

Here are some more details about the spelling rules:

  • Substrings are OK. For example, if we use our bees to spell DELUGE, LUGE is a substring and it makes the Luge Bear happy.
  • The permitted reading orders are top to bottom and left to right (same as Scrabble).
  • We can use the Beehive, the Bearhive, and bears for spelling. In all of these cases, the first letter of the card name is used.
  • Larvas cannot be used for spelling.

Note that the Resourceful mastery is helpful for solving this puzzle, since it allows us to draw more cards and play our Larvae faster.

If we get to the end of Turn 15 and all the bears are happy, we win! See the appendix for one possible solution, containing full details about every turn.

Author’s Notes

When we wrote this puzzle, we thought that Larvae and Bees would Create, subject to the usual constraints. This was later changed so the Larvae and Bees would generate honey at the start of turn, and wouldn’t be unreadied—so now they can “Create” and Special/Flex on the same turn!
