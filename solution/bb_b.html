Solution: bb b

Written by CJ Quines

Unlike most battles, the goal of this one is to lose against bb b. It helps to understand what bb b is doing each turn to make this harder.

In the first turn:

  • bb b Summons five Beeflectors.
    • Unlike a normal Summon, the Beeflectors are on our team, and not bb b’s team.
    • The Beeflector deals 1 Damage to any Creature that Damages it.

Every turn after:

  • For each Reckless Medisaur, bb b tries to use the first Special, and if they can’t, they’ll use the second Special.
    • The first Special deals 2 Damage to bb b’s Base, and removes all Damage from a Creature.
    • Note that bb b can pick any Creature that has taken Damage, including ours! In fact, bb b will prefer to pick our Creatures if possible.
    • The second Special Attacks the nearest Unprotected Enemy Base, or if there isn’t one, the nearest Unprotected Enemy Creature.
  • bb b tries to Summon a Reckless Medisaur in the row in front of their Base.
  • bb b tries to Summon three Beeflectors, preferring to Summon in the rows nearest to them.

We can try to not do anything. If we don’t do anything on our turns, though, we’ll win:

  • On bb b’s second turn, they use the Reckless Medisaur to Attack a Beeflector. This Damages the Beeflector, and also Damages the Reckless Medisaur.
  • On bb b’s third turn, they use the first Reckless Medisaur to heal a Beeflector, and the second Reckless Medisaur to heal the first. This deals 4 Damage to bb b’s Base, so all of bb b’s Bases are Destroyed and we win.

We lose if all our Bases get Destroyed, and the only way for bb b to Damage our Bases is to use Reckless Medisaur. For Medisaur to actually Attack a Base:

  • there must be no Creatures that have taken Damage, and
  • there must be an Unprotected Base. This is harder to get than usual, because bb b adds Beeflectors on our team that Protect the Bases.

This means that if we want to lose by getting bb b to Damage our Bases, we need to have an Unprotected Base. There’s several ways to do this.

  • We can Move the Beeflectors out of the way.
    • We can do this because the Beeflectors are on our team.
    • By Moving Beeflectors to the other side of the field, they become Sneaky, so they no longer Protect our Bases.
    • Moving Beeflectors to the other side of the field also gives us space to Move the new Beeflectors that get Summoned every turn.
    • We have to be careful however; if we block the Medisaurs from being Summoned, there might not be enough incoming damage to lose. Thus, we need to avoid moving Beeflectors to that row.
  • We can Move our Bases into the way.
    • We can do this using the card Switch Boar’d.
    • By using a column of Switch Boar’ds, we can keep Bases in front of other Friendly Units, making them Unprotected from the Reckless Medisaurs.
    • It’s also possible to use the Switch Boar’ds to rearrange the Beeflectors in other ways.

An alternate way is to get ourselves to Destroy our own Bases. I don’t think there’s any way to do this except using Mister Penny, Scrapyard Refurbisher, but it does lead to the fastest solution.
