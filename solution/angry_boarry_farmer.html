Solution: Angry Boarry Farmer

Written by Violet Xiao

Though the Angry Boarry Farmer may seem very intimidating at first, a couple things about the battle can be used to our advantage:

  • The Angry Boarry Farmer can attack only one Unit per turn, and it must attack an Unprotected Unit.
  • The Pepper-Rich Farms on the first and fifth columns also buff our units.

Bringing a deck with many Creatures that cost 1 or 2 Food, we can play our Creatures only in the first and fifth columns, which will get us into a state where every turn, we can play a Creature to replace the one that the Angry Boarry Farmer attacked, and attack with our units in our second row on the first and fifth columns. These two units will grow in power every turn, thanks to the Pepper-Rich Farms, and will be able to take care of the Angry Boarry Farmer before we run out of cards in our deck.

An alternate solution involves playing Ultra Violet (which is unlocked later in the hunt) and enough 1 Food Creatures to safely get it into play, and then using Ultra Violetâ€™s attack to prevent the Angry Boarry Farmer from doing anything.

Another alternate solution involves first attacking down the Train Boar-ding Platform. If this is taken down first, then the Angry Boarry Farmer is much more limited in its movement. We can even lead it on a merry chase between the fourth and fifth (or first and second) columns with one unit that alternates movement every turn, while our other units attack.

Author’s Notes

This battle was a relatively late addition. My hope for it was that it would seem really intimidating at first, but actually serve as an extended tutorial of the game.
