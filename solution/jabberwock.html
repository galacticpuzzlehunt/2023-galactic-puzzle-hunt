Solution: Jabberwock

Written by Brian Chen

In this battle, the enemy Gostaks deal damage to each of our Unprotected Units every turn according to some string of symbols, whose meanings depend on various aspects of the Creature being damaged and which we must determine.

Generally, the goal is to experiment with different Creatures until we understand the symbols’ meanings, then “stabilize” by finding a configuration of Creatures so that our Unprotected Creatures take 0 damage each turn. From there we can either just attack with our Creature repeatedly until the Gostak is defeated, or find ways to optimize and play stronger Protected Creatures.

Gostaks I, II, III

Gostak I introduces “◆”, Power of target, so we can stabilize by playing any 0-Power Creatures in front.

Gostak II introduces “♠”, Health of target (not Current Health, i.e. the number that’s unaffected by Damage). You can stabilize by playing Petroleum Jelly in the middle column. This Gostak’s health is also low enough for us to survive with various other cards that reduce damage and deal enough damage before our Camps are destroyed.

Gostak III introduces “§”, the absolute difference of the next two symbols: here, they are ◆ Power and ♠ Health. It is pretty easy to stabilize since we can play any of the many Creatures with equal Power and Health.

All three fights can be defeated by bringing a deck with 1× Petroleum Jelly, playing it in R4C3, then repeatedly attacking with it over the next 10 turns. To speed things up, we can add 1× Yoshisaur Munchakoopas, playing it in R5C3, and then repeatedly attacking with it over the next 5 Turns.

Gostak IV

This battle introduces “‡”, length of target’s name, as well as a Gostak with a higher Health to encourage more optimization. To stabilize, we want a Creature with §◆‡ = 0, that is, a Creature whose Power equals the length of its name; o fits the bill.

The solution that’s simplest to describe is to bring 1× o, playing it in R4C3, then repeatedly attacking with it over the next 25 turns.

As before, we can play a Yoshisaur Munchakoopas behind it to just do it in 1 + 13 turns.

For even more efficiency, bring 2× o, 1× Glass of Moo or Glass of Moocha, and 1× Queen of Bison. Then:

  1. Turn 1: Play o on R4C1 and R4C3.
  2. Turn 2: Play Glass on R5C3.
  3. Turn 3: Special with Glass and Create with both o’s, then play Queen of Bison etc. on R5C3.
  4. Turn 4, 5, 6: Attack with Queen of Bison.

Gostak V

This battle introduces two new symbols at once: “✠”, the Manhattan distance between the Gostak and the Creature, and “♫”, A1Z26 of the first letter of the target’s color. (These two can’t be disambiguated from each other yet, but the symbols are meant to be slightly semantically suggestive, and there’s no need to disambiguate them until Gostak VII.)

Unlike the previous battles, there is no way to stabilize on the first turn, because there’s no overlap between the Manhattan distances of Spaces where we can play a Creature (3 to 6) and the A1Z26 numbers of the first letters of colors in the game (2=B for Black, 7=G for Green/Gray, 16=P for Purple, 18=R for Red, and 25=Y for Yellow). However, it’s very close on both ends. To stabilize, we can protect a Beeowulf for a turn and move the Gostak one space to the left or right to allow us to play Green or Gray Creatures at a distance of 7, or move the Gostak one space down to allow us to play a Black Creature at a distance of 2. Also, even without moving the Gostak, Green/Gray Creatures at distance 6 and Black Creatures at distance 3 often can survive long enough for us to win anyway.

One solution: Bring 2× Beeowulf, 2× Petroleum Jelly, 1× Yoshisaur Munchakoopas. Then:

  1. Turn 1: Play Beeowulf R4C3, R5C3 and Petroleum Jelly R4C5.
  2. Turn 2: Special with Beeowulf to move the Gostak left. Play Petroleum Jelly R5C5.
  3. Turn 3: Play Yoshisaur Munchakoopas R6C4.
  4. Turn 4 to 16: Attack with Yoshisaur Munchakoopas.

A faster solution: Bring 1× Nice Cow, 1× Beeowulf. Then:

  1. Turn 1: Play Nice Cow R4C3, Beeowulf R5C3.
  2. Turn 2: Special with Beeowulf to move Gostak down.
  3. Turn 3, 4, 5, 6: Create with Beeowulf, then Special with Nice Cow.
  4. Turn 7, 8, 9, 10: Attack with Beeowulf.

Gostak VI

This battle introduces “¶”, the product of the next two symbols, as well as demonstrating that symbols can compose. So this Gostak deals §‡¶✠♫ = absdiff(name length, distance × color A1Z26) damage. Yoshisaur Munchakoopas is 21 letters and Green (G=7), and so can be placed at distance 3 to stabilize.

Solution: Bring 1× Yoshisaur Munchakoopas. Then:

  1. Turn 1: Play Yoshisaur Munchakoopas R4C3.
  2. Turn 2 to 14: Just attack with it repeatedly.

As before, we can put something behind Yoshisaur Munchakoopas to go faster. If we bring 1× Yoshisaur Munchakoopas, two arbitrary 1-drops, 1 Glass of Moo or Glass of Moocha, and 1 Thesaurus, then we can win as follows:

  1. Turn 1: Play Yoshisaur Munchakoopas R4C3 and a 1-drop in front of another Camp.
  2. Turn 2: Create with Yoshisaur Munchakoopas, then play Glass in R5C3 and another 1-drop in front of a Camp.
  3. Turn 3: Create with Yoshisaur Munchakoopas, Special with the Glass, and play Thesaurus in R5C3.
  4. Turn 4 to 9: Attack with Thesaurus.

Gostak VII

The final battle: Each turn, this Gostak does §✠¶♠‡ damage, then ¶∮§♠‡ damage.

The new symbol, “∮”, is turn count, though it’s not necessary to determine its meaning. All we need to know (or guess) is that it can’t be 0, so to stabilize, we need to make the other factor it gets multiplied with 0. That is, we want §✠¶♠‡ = 0 and §♠‡ = 0, which means ♠ = ‡ and ✠ = ♠×‡ is a square. We can place re at distance 4 and buff it with o to 2 Health to stabilize.

Solution: Bring 2× re and 1× o. Play re at R5C3 and R4C4, then play o at R5C4. (The o must be played last.) Then just repeatedly attack with them.

We can also Create enough Food with these Creatures to bring a Rude Cow. Bring 1× o, 2× re, 1× Rude Cow, then:

  1. Turn 1: Play re on R4C4, re on R5C3, o on R5C4.
  2. Turn 2: Create with all Creatures and play Rude Cow on R6C4.
  3. Turn 3 and onwards: Attack with Rude Cow.
