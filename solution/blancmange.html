Solution: Blancmange

Written by Charles Tam

In this puzzle battle, the following rule modifications are in effect:

  • All cards whose names start with a K, N, P, Q, R, or B are modified by this puzzle. We will refer to these cards as pieces.
  • Pieces come into play with a +1 Power buff to hint that they are special.
  • Pieces use the attack rules of the chess piece signified by their first letter. They ignore the card game’s "protection" mechanic, but can be blocked by friendly or enemy units along their attack path. When a piece attacks and destroys a unit, it automatically moves to the space the destroyed unit occupied.
    • Boarry Farmer’s column-only attack restriction is silently replaced its diagonal-only attack restriction.
  • Piece movement is unaffected; they still only move one space orthogonally.
  • P-pieces are automatically replaced by Quail Aboard Fiery Steeds when they enter the opposing player’s back row.

In order to solve the puzzle, when the opponent’s Camp Keep is destroyed, its space and all touching spaces must either be under attack by pieces, or blocked by the opponent’s own Units.

Like most puzzles, the opponent disregards any sense of economic fairness, and instead automatically summons Units just to make life difficult:

  • Before the player’s first turn, the opponent spawns a line of Petroleum Jellys in the row in front of its Keep.
  • Before the player’s third turn, the opponent spawns three Vanilla Calfs in the 2nd, 3rd, and 4th cells in the row in front of its Keep.
  • Before the player’s fifth turn, the opponent spawns two Bell Flowers in the back corners of the board, and two more in the two spaces in front of them.
  • Before the player’s seventh turn, the opponent spawns a line of Petroleum Jellys in the row in front of its Keep.
  • Before the player’s eighth turn, the opponent spawns two Realistic Rexes in the back row of the board, priortizing the corners.
  • Before the player’s tenth turn, and every three player turns thereafter, the opponent spawns two Realistic Rexes, two Bell Flowers, and a Quail Aboard Fiery Steeds in its back row. (The last spawn doesn't usually happen due to the Keep occupying the spot.)

Note that opponent spawns can usually be blocked by having a unit in the place where it wants to spawn, so "capturing" a piece on one of its two back rows not only removes the existing piece but also can prevent a reinforcement from appearing.

Opponent pieces attack if able, and otherwise move in a way that best pressures the player Keep. Note that opponent Petroleum Jellys do promote into Quail Aboard Fiery Steeds if they’re allowed to reach the player back rank.

The main challenge in this battle is getting enough Units into play to contest the tide of free units the opponent produces. There are a few ways to do this, but here is a Yellow/Green/Purple deck that can win without using Legendaries, along with hints on how to use them:

  • Bee Rider
    • A Bishop that gets to adjust its position for free each turn.
  • Beethoven
    • Good for tanking hits while we get everything else set up.
  • Bell Flower
    • A Bishop that can one-hit everything except Rooks.
  • Chicken
    • Food economy; keep it in the back ranks and use it for Pie whenever it doesn’t have anything better to do.
  • Dino Nugget
    • Just a 1-cost card that sometimes makes our other chaff harder to kill while we get set up.
  • Eager Beever
    • An easy source of Honey for our other Yellow cards. Use it to body-block the opponent’s Pawns, or in late game to cheat one of our own Pawns across the board.
  • Flora the Explora
    • Card draw, 1-cost Flower source.
  • Nam & Pam Bee
    • A Knight that can one-hit everything except Rooks.
  • Nightshade
    • This Unit’s Special is not an attack and is not restricted by the chess gimmick. Good for converting unwanted cards into damage.
  • PBee & J
    • Cheap source of Honey; also okay for blocking or trading against the opponent’s Pawns while we get set up.
  • Petroleum Jelly
    • Cheap source of Eggs; also good for getting through the initial Pawn wave and menacing the Vanilla Calfs behind it.
  • Realistic Rex
    • If we can get this out on our second turn, it’s an efficient way to clean up a lot of the opponent’s unit spam. Keep in mind, though, that when using its Special both its targets must be chosen from its initial position (rather than choosing the second target predicated on its successful "capture" of the first).
  • Yoshisaur Munchakoopas
    • Just here to shore up any targeting or tanking weaknesses.

Restart the battle until we draw a hand containing three 1-cost cards, preferably including a Chicken.

On turn 1, play the Chicken to one of our back corners and the other two 1-cost cards to your front corners.

On turn 2, if you have Realistic Rex in hand, first use one of our existing Units to Create, and destroy a Pawn with another. Then Create with the third unit, and summon Realistic Rex somewhere it won’t get attacked. Otherwise, use the front units to knock out the Pawns threatening them, and use the Chicken to either get

a 2-cost unit onto the board or to generate more energy for next turn.

After this, the strategic points are:

  • Eliminate threats to board development (i.e. remove as many enemy pieces as possible). Ideally, we get Bishops or Knights – all of which have 3 Power including the puzzle-specific Power buff – into positions where they can knock out Vanilla Calfs (turn 3) or Bell Flowers (turn 5) as soon as they spawn.
  • Ignore the opponent’s side Petroleum Jellys. Block them with small Units, which will generally be used to Create food instead of attacking. As long as we don’t sit a unit on their one attackable square, they’re not a threat.
  • At the beginning, 1-cost summons are good for Creating food on a subsequent turn. The main exception is Yoshisaur Munchakoopas, who is not constrained by chess geometry the way the most of our other units will be.
  • The win condition is to place a Bishop, Pawn, or Knight in a place where it can deal a killing blow to the enemy Keep. Beethoven on the second row is especially good at staying alive a turn longer than usual due to its defensive ability. On our tenth turn, after the opponent fills its first two rows, use this unit to deliver a smothered mate, using its reinforcements against it.

If we have access to bb b, brave as can b, we can add it to feed off all the early Pawns we’ll be destroying. In this case, we probably won’t need to use any tricks and can win just by having more Power and units. The hardest part will be getting our units in position to checkmate without getting interrupted by reinforcements. Using Eager Beever to teleport a Pawn or Rook onto the opponent’s back rank helps a lot.
